MISSION AND VISION STATEMENTS

Our Mission

The mission of our project of generating electricity from exothermic reactions is to explore sustainable and innovative ways to harness energy. By focusing on converting the heat released from exothermic reactions into usable electrical energy, your project aims to contribute to the development of alternative and renewable energy sources. It can also highlight the importance of utilizing waste heat from industrial processes to generate electricity efficiently. This project can promote environmental sustainability and energy efficiency.

Our Vision

Our vision is to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and promote cleaner energy sources. This project aims to inspire innovation in the field of energy generation and contribute to a greener environment for future generations. By advancing sustainable energy solutions and fostering a culture of environmental responsibility, we strive to make a significant impact on reducing carbon emissions and combating climate change. Through collaboration, research, and development, we are committed to creating a sustainable future where clean energy is accessible, affordable, and efficient for everyone.

INSIGHTS OF THE DESIGN THINKING



OUR PROJECT - THE THERMO-VOLT

When reactants are mixed together, collisions between atoms take place. In 1880, Svante Arrhenius proposed that the atoms must posses some minimum amount of energy for a reaction to occur which is called the threshold energy. At times this energy is already possessed in the molecules and no external agent is required for the reaction to begin. However, sometimes the reaction to begin some external energy is required to be given which is known as the activation energy. This energy is required to break the pre-existing bonds of the compounds. Once the pre-existing bonds are broken, new bonds tend to form with the creation of new products. This process tends to absorb energy. Hence a conclusion can be drawn that a chemical reaction involves the release and subsequent absorption of energy. At times, there is an imbalance of energy during the reaction which causes energy to be either released or absorbed. Endothermic reaction – When the threshold energy is greater than the released energy endothermic reaction occurs. Exothermic reaction – When the threshold energy is less than the released energy exothermic reaction occurs. We have thoroughly researched and found out that,

Fe2O3 + 2Al -> 2Fe+ Al2O3

This is a exothermic reaction which releases heat in a very large amount, According to enthalpic calculations, 1 mole of the reaction produces 851.5 KJ of heat is produced. To boil 100 litres of water, approximately, 47.9 kg Rust is required to combine with 16.18 kg of Aluminium. 100 kg of steam will produce approximately 115,700 kJ (or 115.7 MJ) of energy in a steam turbine with an efficiency of 85%. Our project aims to generate electricity from the heat produced due to exothermic chemical reactions.
